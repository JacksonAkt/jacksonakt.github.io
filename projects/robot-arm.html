6-Axis Industrial Manipulator

System Integration & Power Electronics

Role

Lead Engineer

Hardware

  • Arduino Mega 2560
  • 6x NEMA Stepper Motors
  • 25kg High-Torque Servo
  • A4988 Drivers

Power

24V / 15A Switching PSU

Technical Challenge

The primary challenge was managing the high inductive noise and current draw of a 6-axis system. The 25kg servo used for the end-effector generated significant back-EMF, which threatened the stability of the Arduino logic rail.

Engineering Spotlight: Power Stability

To resolve voltage sag during simultaneous multi-axis movement, I integrated a 16V 1000 µF reservoir capacitor. This successfully buffered the servo's peak current demands, maintaining a clean signal floor for the MCU.

Thermal Management

Operating the NEMA motors at 24V pushed the A4988 drivers to their thermal limits. I implemented a forced-air cooling solution consisting of:

Aluminum Heatsinks

Dual 40mm Fans

Control Logic

The firmware was developed to decode IR signals in real-time. Using the Mega's hardware timers, I synchronized step-pulse generation across 6 axes, ensuring smooth motion profiles and manual override capabilities via remote.
